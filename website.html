Five Kinds of Fruits

by: Caloy

Si Caloy ay magbibigay ng kaalaman tungkol sa 5 na klase ng prutas.

  1. Apple(Mansanas)

    • The origin of apples traces back to the Middle East over 4,000 years ago.

    • A real-life Granny Smith discovered the apple now known as Granny Smith.

    • The Red Delicious apple tree was discovered in Iowa.

    • It took over 30 years to develop the Honeycrisp apple.

    • The origin of apples traces back to the Middle East over 4,000 years ago.

  2. Banana(Saging)

    • The banana fruit got its name after being sold wrapped in aluminum foil for 10 cents at the 1876 Philadelphia World Fair.

    • The classic banana species we consumed was called the Gros Michel.

    • The banana is scientifically a berry, whereas the strawberry is not.

    • Bananas are a healthy source of fiber, potassium, vitamin B6, vitamin C, and various antioxidants and phytonutrients.

    • The oils inside a banana skin can actually help inflammation and itching caused by insect bites.

  3. Grapes(Ubas)

    • Grapefruit is a citrus fruit with a flavor that can range from bittersweet to sour.

    • The grapefruit first appeared in the 18th century, as a result of crossing a pomelo and an orange

    • The nutrients grapefruit contains may help promote healthy skin and protect against various conditions. They may also play a role in weight maintenance.

    • Grapefruit is low on the glycemic index. This means that it provides nutrients but does not have a significant negative impact on a person's blood sugar levels.

    • Grapefruit comes in varieties of white, pink, and red, all of which are incredibly beneficial to human health

  4. Mango(Mangga)

    • Botanically, mango is a drupe, consisting of an outer skin, a fleshy edible portion, and a central stone enclosing a single seed - also called stone fruit, like a plum, cherry, or peach.

    • Where do mangos come from? Mangos were first grown in India over 5,000 years ago.

    • Mangos are related to cashews and pistachios.

    • A ripe mango is known to be 14% sugar by weight and 0.5% acid by weight, with a sugar acid ratio of 28.

    • Mango bark, leaves, skin, flesh, and the pit have been used in folk remedies for centuries.

  5. Tangerine(Dalanghita)

    • The tangerine is actually a variety of the Mandarin Orange, which is native to Southeastern Asia.

    • Tangerines are also known for their bright orange peel, which is usually much more vibrant than the peel of an orange.

    • The pulp of tangerines is also extremely abundant in Vitamin C.

    • A tangerine tree is quite interesting because it is much smaller than most of the other citrus fruits which tend to be on large side.

    • These small citrus fruits are packed with high water content (85%), consist mostly of carbs, and contain almost no fat.
